# Summary: Worst Practices in Search Engine Optimization
**Author:** Ross A. Malaga  
**Source:** Communications of the ACM, Vol. 51, No. 12 (December 2008)

## Overview
This article explores "black hat" Search Engine Optimization (SEO) techniques—methods used to manipulate search engine rankings that violate search engine guidelines. While these tactics can yield short-term gains, they often result in penalties or outright bans from search indices. The author argues that legitimate "white hat" optimizers must understand these techniques to protect their own rankings, avoid inadvertent penalties, and adapt useful strategies in ethical ways.

## The Importance of Ranking
With 62% of users clicking only on results from the first Search Engine Results Page (SERP), ranking well is critical for online visibility. "Black hat" practitioners often exploit this by generating thousands of temporary, high-ranking sites to push legitimate sites down, or by stealing content from others.

## Black Hat Techniques

### 1. Indexing Tricks
*   **Blog-ping (BP):** A technique to attract search engine spiders quickly.
    *   **Method:** Establish hundreds or thousands of blogs, post a link to a target site on each, and continuously "ping" blog servers to signal updates.
    *   **Result:** Search spiders are attracted to the activity and follow the links to the new site.
    *   **White Hat Adaptation:** Legitimate optimizers may use a moderate version of this (posting a link on a few blogs and pinging on updates) to speed up indexing.

### 2. On-Page Techniques
These methods often involve showing one version of content to search engines (to rank high) and another to users (for usability or conversion).
*   **Cloaking:** Detecting the IP address of the requestor. If it is a known search engine spider, an optimized (often text-only) page is served. If it is a human user, the normal site is served.
*   **Doorway Pages:** Creating separate pages optimized for specific keywords. These pages often use a "fast meta refresh" to instantly redirect human users to the main content pages, while search engines index the optimized "doorway."
*   **Invisible Content:** Hiding text (keywords) from users while making it visible to spiders.
    *   **Old Methods:** Same foreground/background color, tiny fonts.
    *   **New Methods:** Using CSS and hidden `div` tags. Search engines like Google now penalize or ignore content in hidden divs.
*   **Content Generators:** Automated tools that scrape the web for keywords to compile "new" pages. This results in duplicate content, which search engines often penalize.

### 3. Off-Page Techniques
Search engines rely heavily on the number and quality of incoming links (backlinks) to determine rank. Black hats manipulate this via:
*   **Guest Book Spamming:** Posting comments with links on authoritative sites (like `.edu` or `.gov` domains) to boost credibility.
*   **Link Farms:** Groups of pages created solely to link to one another, often generated by automated tools.
*   **HTML Injection:** Exploiting security vulnerabilities in web search programs (e.g., WebGlimpse) to insert links into another site’s search results or pages.

### 4. Bowling the Competition
A destructive tactic aimed at lowering a competitor's ranking or getting them banned.
*   **Method:** A black hat optimizer artificially associates a competitor's site with "bad neighborhoods" (e.g., link farms, gambling, or adult sites) or injects repetitive keywords (spam) into the competitor's site via HTML injection.
*   **Goal:** Trigger a search engine penalty for the competitor, thereby allowing the black hat's site to rise in the rankings.

## Recommendations for SEO Strategy
The author advises caution when hiring SEO consultants, as "black hat" actions taken on your behalf can lead to your site being banned.
1.  **Check the Consultant’s Rank:** If they cannot rank well for "search engine optimization," they likely cannot help you.
2.  **Define Keywords Yourself:** Don't rely solely on the consultant; ensure the targets are relevant and competitive.
3.  **Maintain Control:** Do not give full control of the site to a consultant.
4.  **Get References:** Verify past results.

**Conclusion:** Webmasters cannot ignore black hat SEO. It poses a direct threat through competition and "bowling," but also offers technical insights that, when moderated, can ethically improve site visibility.
